MULETHI , YASHTIMADHU [ GLYCYRRHIZA GLABRA]

Its english name is licorice or Mulethi in Hindi. It is cultivated in some parts of india, it is rich in medicinal properties. It has an antioxidant, Antacid, antimicrobial, Antiallergic, anti-inflammatory, Antiulcer, Expectorant, Laxative and hepatoprotective properties. It reduces stomach acid secretion, it shows good effects in acidity, indigestion and ulcer prevention, and also boosts immunity. It also gives Soothing effect on the skin. According to Ayurveda it is jivaniya (vitalising), Kanthya (good for throat), kandughna (removes itching).

Part used – Roots

AYURVEDIC PROPERTIES –

  1. Rasa (taste on the tongue): Madhura (Sweet), Tikta (Bitter)
  2. Guna : Guru (Heavy), Snigdha (Unctuous)
  3. Virya (Action): Shita (Cooling),
  4. Vipaka – Madhura (Sweet)

EFFECT ON DOSHA – IT soothes all the three dosha

DOSAGE – Licorice Churna – ¼-½ teaspoon twice a day.

Licorice Capsule – 1-2 capsules twice a day.

Licorice Tablet – 1-2 tablets twice a day.

USES –

  1. Hypertension
  2. Liver diseases
  3. Heart disesases
  4. Diabetes
  5. Jaundice
  6. Ulcerative colitis
  7. Weakness
  8. Bronchitis
