HADJOD [ cissus quadrangularis ]

The next important herb in our Ayurvedic herbs list is Hadjob. It is commonly known as Asthishrinkhala. According to Ayurveda, It is used as a specific for fractures of bones. Found in India or Sri Lanka, Africa, Arabia . It reduces swelling and fracture pain. Hadjod having properties like Analgesic, Antioxidant, Antinflammatory, Anti -ulcer, Anti-microbial. It can be used both internally and externally. It also good for the eyes and increase appetite.

Part used – Juice of stem and leaves, dried powder

AYURVEDIC PROPERTY –

Rasa (taste on the tongue): Katu (Pungent), Madhura (Sweet)

Guna (Pharmacological Action): Laghu (Light), Ruksha (Dry), Sara (Unstable)

Virya (Action): Ushna (Heating)

Vipaka (transformed state after digestion): Katu (Pungent)

EFFECT ON DOSHA – Increase pitta, balances vatta and kaffa dosha

DOSAGE –Decoction of dried stem: 10-30 ml, Stem Juice: 10-20 ml, Stem Powder – 3-6g

USES –

  1. Osteoarthritis
  2. Osteoporosis
  3. Obesity
  4. Menopause
  5. Leprosy
  6. Dyspepsia
  7. Fracture
  8. Joints problems
